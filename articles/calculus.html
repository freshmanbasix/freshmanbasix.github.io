GEOMETRY

Geometry is the mathematical field concerned with space, dimensions, shapes, size and distances.

COORDINATE SYSTEMS

A coordinate system is a organized set of numerical values representing a point in a space of some dimensions. Three such system are the Cartesian, cylindrical and Spehrical coordinate systems. Different systems have differing benefits and drawbacks, mainly depending on what is being depicted, and what kind of operations are being performed.

CARTESIAN COORDINATES

Cartesian Coordinates is the common standard of representing space by letting each point in P = [x1, ..., xn] ‚àà ‚Ñùn be represented by a multiple of 1 on n orthoganal axis. The regular xy-plane is often refered to as the cartesian plane. Euclidean Standard Space ‚Ñù3, i.e physical 3D space, is usually represented as [x, y, z] in cartesian coordinates.

It is used as the basis as the common right-hand system, which is a method to standardize direction in a system. It is defined by taking your right hand and letting:

  • the thumb be the positive x-axis
  • the index finger be the positive y-axis
  • the long finger be the positive z-axis
  • The circular rotation around the z-axis, from the positive x-axis to the negative, is considered to be of a positive magnitude

Positive rotation around the z axis represents position movement along the z-axis. A common example is the common threaded screw, which is screwed into a material by rotating it clockwise when the screw faces away from you.

POLAR COORDINATES

In a polar system, a ray between the point P is set as a polar axis, and the line through that pole has a single angle. The magnitude of this line from point to origin and the angle make up the polar coordinates. This system is usually best used for Cylindrical [r, Œ∏, z] and Spherical [R, Œ∏, œï] objects in space. This can be used to very easily calculate points of a spherical or cylindrical surface, or determine volumes etc.

TRIGONOMETRY

Trigonometry is the field pertaining to periodical functions.

TRIGONOMETRIC FUNCTIONS

The Trigonometric Functions describe real mathematical relationships between the hypotenuse and one of the sides of a right-angle triangle. By this definition, it maps the non-right angle in the triangle to a corresponding numerical value.

The primary functions are the sine, cosine and tangent functions.

They each have respective reciprocal functions secant, cosecant and cotangent.

The reverse concept of translating a real numerical value to an angle is achieved with the arc function.
