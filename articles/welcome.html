Welcome to the Freshman Basix Wiki for computer science tinkerers. This is a resource intended to help interested parties get familiar with, or reaquaint themselves with computer science topics. There will be a particular focus on hands on implementation with a strong prescence of theoretical background. Read below for more background, or choose a topic on the left to read more.

History

Freshman Basix had its origin at The Royal Institute of Technology(KTH) in 2015, when David Jacobsson and David Henriksson jokingly created the company. The name is derived partly from the joke that the tasks undertaken by the company was at the first year basic level, and also that university freshmen are basic in the derogatory sense

Through the years, many uncompleted project ideas saw the light of day, and the idea was born. What if there was a single place where all the knowledge needed to start fidgeting with any of these ideas was gathered. As such, the Freshman Basix Wiki was created to house content authored by mainly its creators and contain part lecture notes, part summaries of googling sessions and part formal documentation that may help anyone orient themselves when starting to get their feet wet in the field of computers, hardware, information technology and all the rest.

This wiki is taking its first studdering steps in the summer of 2021. Hopefully, the content within these pages will prove to be inspiring, useful and continue to grow for many years to come.

Kista Mentorspace

For many years now, there has been a hidden gem stashed away at the KTH satellite campus in Kista. Run by the inimitable Mark Smith, the mentorspace offers tools, equipment and knowledge for anyone who would like to get a project going. To complement the resources on offer in the mentorspace, this wiki also aims to be a shared hub of additional documentation that pertains to projects, workshops or topics being worked on in the mentorspace.

Contact or Contribute

In the spirit of the rag-tag-beginner skillset that epitomizes the origin of this wiki, this website is a pretty clumsy hodge-podge of decreasingly banal efforts, always striving to become less trivial. Should you feel the desire to correct, complement or contribute to this wiki, feel free to smash that pull-request button over on the website github page. Alternatively, you can send an email.
