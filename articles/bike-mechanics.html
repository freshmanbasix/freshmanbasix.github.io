TERMINOLOGY

A Cog is a tooth attached to some kind of wheel. A Cogwheel is any kind of wheel that has teeth(i.e cogs). A Sprocket is a wheel with teeth that that mesh with some kind of perforated or indented material, e.g a chain or belt. The difference here is somewhat semantic, but when discussing any or either of these terms in relation to a bike, one is discussing either the whole drivetrain or a single step on it.

WHEELS

SPOKES

Spokes consist of the metal spoke itself, which on one end is flattened to fasten it to the hub. On the other end it is threaded. The nipple is a nut with threads on the inside. When looking through the rim, into the hub of the wheel, a clockwise rotation of the nipple will tighten it.
